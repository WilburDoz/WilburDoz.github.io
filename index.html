Not-so-serious Things

Welcome, web surfer, to my little corner of the interwebs.

Perhaps you arrived here with curiosity about my professional life?

  • I am a PhD student at the Gatsby Computational Neuroscience Unit, part of UCL. I'm working with Tim Behrens on biological structure learning, and Peter Latham on neural networks.
  • I used to be an intern at OIST (Okinawa Instute of Science and Technology) in Prof. Erik de Schutter's group. I worked on biologically-plausible reinforcement learning.
  • I attended the 2020 Imbizo computational neuroscience summer school.
  • I first met theoretical neuroscience working for Prof. Cengiz Pehlevan at Harvard. I worked on theoretical olfaction.
  • I used to be a condensed matter physicist, and when I first moved to Harvard I worked on acoustic metamaterial mimics of condensed matter systems in Prof. Jenny Hoffman's lab.
  • I did a summer undergrad project on biophysics working in Prof. Aravi Samuel's lab. This was part of the PRISE programme, that I later became a proctor for.
  • I did my undergrad in physics at Cambridge.

If you like this kind of stuff may I point you to a collection of my research projects? Or my CV? Perhaps even my Google Scholar?

However, I mainly became a proud website owner in order to host wacky things on the internet, you can find these in the 'Not-so-serious Things' section.
